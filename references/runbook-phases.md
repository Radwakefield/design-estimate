# Runbook phases & building blocks

Source: Project runbook v3. The estimate's project plan is composed of building blocks
that map onto these five lifecycle phases.

## The five phases
1. **Alignment** - Design Kick-off -> Audience Jobs. Goal: align on problem, scope, ambition
   before committing to execution. Reviewers: DPM, DD.
2. **Divergent Exploration** - Design Exploration & Crit (Divergent). Goal: explore a broad
   range of solutions; validate problem-solution fit. Reviewers: DL/GDM.
3. **Validation** - Build a UXR plan -> Pre-UXR Design Review. Goal: align on solution +
   constraints before investing in a study. Reviewers: DL/GDM/DD/UXR.
4. **Convergent Refinement** - Design Exploration & Crit (Convergent). Goal: synthesize
   research into a single high-confidence solution. Reviewers: DPM, DL/GDM.
5. **Commitment** - Final Review -> ENG Hand-off. Goal: secure go/no-go and hand a clear,
   organized solution to engineering. Reviewers: EM/IC ENG, PD.

## Building blocks (the atoms of a project plan)
- **Discovery UXR** - generative research to reduce ambiguity (Large+ only).
- **Explore** - divergent design exploration of multiple directions.
- **Critique** - structured feedback at divergence and/or convergence (async or live).
- **Refine** - converge and tighten a direction.
- **Validation UXR** - evaluative research to test a converged solution.
- **Review** - structured alignment moment with Product/Design/Eng leadership.

## Crit placement (from Design Crits @ Rula)
Crits have the most value at two inflection points:
- **Divergence:** after exploring concepts, before converging.
- **Convergence:** after converging + validating, before final refinement for review.
